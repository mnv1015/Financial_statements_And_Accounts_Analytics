# Financial Statements & Account Analytics Report (Power BI)

## Overview
This project presents an interactive **Financial Statements and Account Analytics Power BI Report** designed to transform structured accounting data into meaningful insights on **profitability, financial position, and cash flow performance**.

The report enables stakeholders to monitor **revenue trends, cost structure, margins, and liquidity** through dynamic visuals and standardized financial statement views.

---

## Key Objectives
- Convert raw accounting and ERP data into **standard financial statements**
- Provide **year-wise comparison and variance analysis**
- Deliver **clear visibility into profitability, balance sheet strength, and cash movement**
- Support **data-driven financial decision-making**

---

## Report Structure

### 1. Home
- Central navigation interface for financial report sections  
- Clean entry point for structured exploration  

### 2. Profit & Loss Analysis
- KPI indicators for:
  - **Gross Revenue**
  - **Net Revenue**
  - **Contribution Margin**
  - **EBITDA**
  - **Net Profit %**
- **Year vs Last Year comparison** with variance indicators  
- **Bridge-style visual** explaining movement from revenue to net profit  
- Toggle between **Table view and Bridge chart** for detailed analysis  

### 3. Balance Sheet
- Structured hierarchy of:
  - **Assets**
  - **Liabilities**
  - **Equity**
- Current vs non-current classification  
- Multi-year financial position comparison  

### 4. Cash Flow
- Monthly **bank balance trend**
- **Cash inflow vs outflow** analysis  
- Visibility into **net cash movement and liquidity**

---

## Filters & Interactivity
- **Year selection** (multi-year comparison)
- **Comparison mode** (Budget vs Last Year)
- Dynamic slicing across all report pages
- Drill-down to **account-level financial insights**

---

## Tech Stack
- **Power BI Desktop** – Visualization & report design  
- **Power Query** – Data cleaning and transformation  
- **DAX** – Financial KPIs, variance logic, and calculations  
- **Excel / ERP (Business Central) Data** – Source financial dataset  
- **Data Modeling** – Structured relationships for financial reporting  

---

## Data Sources
Financial transaction-level and account-level data sourced from:
- ERP / Business Central exports  
- Excel-based accounting records  

Includes:
- Revenue & expense transactions  
- Financial statement account classifications  
- Year-wise balances  
- Cash inflow and outflow records

## Business Impact
- Enables **fast evaluation of profitability and margins**
- Provides **clear financial position visibility**
- Highlights **key revenue drivers and cost contributors**
- Improves **liquidity monitoring and planning**
- Reduces dependency on **manual financial reporting**
